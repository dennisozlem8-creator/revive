He was taken to the orthopedic clinic, put in a cast for six weeks, and when the cast came off, his physical therapist handed him a single piece of paper.Dennis at the orthopedic clinic, June 2025X-ray of Dennis's right wrist fracture (Jun 2025)The Paper Exercise SheetThe paper sheet listed six wrist exercises — all Dennis needed to do to recover. His physical therapist told him: do these 5 sessions per day, come back in two weeks, and let me know how it goes.Dennis went home. He did the exercises sometimes. Some days he forgot. Some days he did fewer reps than prescribed. Some days he skipped entirely. When his physical therapist asked at the next appointment how the exercises were going, Dennis said: 'Good, I've been doing them every day.'The critical problem:He hadn't. And there was no way for anyone to know. The paper sheet had no tracking. The exercises had no angle measurement. Nobody could verify whether the reps were correct, whether the hold time was right, or whether recovery was actually happening. Dennis was on his own.Dennis's actual PT exercise sheet from the orthopedic clinic — the 'paper system' Revive Motion replacesDennis shared this with his brother Michael, and they both realized: this is a problem that millions of people face. Athletes, elderly patients, children in physical therapy — everyone gets sent home with a paper sheet and nobody verifies compliance. They decided to build a solution."The doctor gave me the paper and said see you in two weeks. Nobody checked whether I was doing the exercises right. We built Revive Motion so that could never happen to anyone else."— Dennis Ozlem, co-creator of Revive MotionSection 2 — Dennis's PT Exercise ProgramThe Six Wrist Exercises — What Dennis Was PrescribedThe following exercises were prescribed by Dennis's physical therapist (Dr. Michael Reynolds) after his wrist fracture. The findings were significant:76% of physical therapy patients do not complete their home exercise programs (Ohio State Wexner Medical Center, 2026)3.5 million youth sports injuries occur every year in the United States (Safe Kids Worldwide, 2023)Approximately 1.1 million joint replacement surgeries are performed annually (American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons)Falls are the number one cause of injury-related death in adults over 65 (CDC Injury Center, 2024)Patients who don't complete PT exercises take 40% longer to recover and have 60% higher re-injury ratesWhy Existing Solutions FailCurrent ApproachWhy It FailsPaper exercise logsPatients fill from memory. Not continuous monitoring.Section 4 — Purpose and HypothesisPurposeThe purpose of this project is to design and build a wearable sensor device and mobile app that automatically tracks physical therapy exercises in real time — measuring joint angle, rep count, hold time, heart rate, and muscle activation — and reports compliance data to caregivers and physicians without requiring the patient to self-report.HypothesisWe hypothesize that:If a patient uses the Revive Motion wearable sensor and app during physical therapy exercises, then their exercise compliance rate will increase compared to traditional self-reporting (we predict 70%+ vs 50%), and the sensor system will measure joint angles within 5 degrees of a clinical goniometer in at least 80% of trials.VariablesVariable TypeDescriptionIndependentWhether the patient uses Revive Motion (sensor + app) or traditional paper log self-reporting.Dependent (Primary)Physical therapy exercise compliance rate: percentage of prescribed exercises actually completed.Dependent (Secondary)Joint angle measurement accuracy: difference in degrees between sensor reading and goniometer.ControlledSame exercises, same target angle Hypothesis predicted 70%+. We exceeded this.Hypothesis 2 — SUPPORTED:92.5% of sensor readings within 5° of goniometer. Average error 2.8°. Hypothesis required 80%. We exceeded this.Key FindingsAutomatic tracking is far more effective than self-reporting. Patients exercise more when they know it is being verified.The amber 'ALMOST THERE!' state was more motivating than red. Several users said red felt like failure but amber felt like encouragement.The kids avatar speech bubble was the top motivator for child users — mentioned by all 5 child testers.The caregiver status card eliminated daily phone check-in calls for all 5 caregiver participants.Spanish mode rated 4.9/5 — identified as critical for accessibility by Spanish-speaking families.Repositioning the sensor increases error from 2.3° to 4.1° — consistent placement is critical.Sources of ErrorSensor placement: armband repositioned differently each session can shift readings 1-4 degrees.Sample size: 20 participants is not statistically sufficient. A larger study is needed for clinical validation.WiFi latency: occasional 100-200ms delay does not affect compliance tracking but causes brief display lag.Section 11 — Future WorkVersion 1.5 — Near-TermAdd alignment marker on armband to standardize sensor placement and reduce repositioning errorOffline mode: buffer sessions in TinyDB during WiFi outage, sync to CloudDB when reconnectedExpand exercise library to 10+ exercises for all 4 joints: knee, ankle, elbow, wristRecovery milestone celebrations at 50%, 75%, and 100% of ROM goalVersion 2.0 — Long-TermClinical validation: 50+ participants at a physical therapy clinic over 6 weeksTeachable Machine: train AI model to recognize correct/incorrect form from phone cameraMulti-patient PT dashboard: physical therapist monitors all patients from one screenEHR integration: generate structured clinical reports for electronic health recordsCustom 3D-printed sensor housing for precise alignment every sessionSection 12 — ReferencesResearch PapersFranco, E., et al.
