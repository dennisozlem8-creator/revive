Revive MotionMentor BriefSmart Wearable Recovery Monitoring That Connects Home Exercise to Faster HealingTeam: Dennis and Michael Ozlem · Grade 7 · Brandon, FLCompetitions: Congressional App Challenge 2026 · Global Appathon 2027 · STEM Fair 2026–27Platform: MIT App Inventor · Arduino Nano · 3 sensors · CloudDB · AI APIThe Problem76% of physical therapy patients do not complete their home exercise programs. No one is watching when they do their exercises at home. Physical therapists prescribe a plan, patients go home, and compliance is measured only by self-reporting. Missed exercises mean slower recovery, longer treatment, and higher healthcare costs. Injuries affect everyone: 3.5 million youth sports injuries per year, 1.1 million joint replacements annually, and falls are the number-one injury-related cause of death in adults over 65.The gap in care:Between the PT office and the patient's living room, there is no monitoring, no feedback, and no accountability. Revive Motion closes that gap.The SolutionRevive Motion is a wearable sensor device paired with a mobile app that automatically tracks physical therapy exercises in real time. When a patient bends their knee, the device measures the joint angle, counts the rep, tracks how long they held the position, and monitors their heart rate and muscle activation. The app gives instant feedback on form, keeps caregivers informed without phone calls, and sends a weekly AI-generated report to the physician.The DeviceSensorMeasuresWorn onMPU-6050 GyroscopeJoint angle, reps, hold time, range of motionKnee, ankle, elbow, or wrist via CGM-style neoprene armbandMAX30102 Heart Rate SensorExercise effort and intensity in real timeInner wrist, pressed against skin through pocket openingMyoWare 2.0 EMG SensorMuscle activation — confirms correct muscle firingDirectly on muscle belly with adhesive electrode padsAll three sensors connect wirelessly to the MIT App Inventor app via WiFi.
